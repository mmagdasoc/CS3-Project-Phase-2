Overview of His Proposals, Programs, and Actions

Rodrigo Duterte’s presidency began at the noontime of June 30, 2016 and is expected to end at June 30, 2022. He garnered 16 million votes or about 39% of the total votes. Let’s take a look on how his presidency affected this country. What was his advocations and proposals and did it even do good for the overall welfare of the Philippines?




Perpetuating an Oligarchic Rule

During his populist campaign for president in 2015 and 2016, Duterte took aim at the corruption and excesses of wealth-hoarding ruling families. But, he, himself, chose to ally with traditional corrupt politicians, big business owners, and landlords. One example of this is how he manipulated everything and threw senseless allegations at Globe and Smart for the benefit of the Davao crony Dennis Uy and his company who in 2018 was awarded, without public tender, a national telecom license. This company, now known as Dito, was once a small local Muslim outfit, but is now owned 60 percent by Uy’s companies and 40 percent by the state-owned China Telecom. It so happens that Duterte’s threats against Globe and Smart are due to take effect at the end of this year, just when the Dito network is supposed to be up and running. Dito thus represents a convergence of presidential cronyism with Duterte’s preference for promoting the interests of China’s state companies. The country's economy also doubled in size in 20 years yet the wealth inequality is the 9th worst globally (18 billionares today; 3 billionares, 10 years ago) as noted by Credit Suisse.




Promoting Hate Speech, Bigotry, and Misinformation

It is no secret that he is very sexist and misogynist. He made numerous rape jokes. One of the things he said was, "I was angry because she was raped, that's one thing- but she was so beautiful, the mayor should have been first,” Duterte said at a Quezon City rally, referring to himself. “I looked at her face - what a waste.” Duterte also decided to tell the crowd about something he did as a teenager in university. Recounting the time he entered a maid’s room while she was sleeping, he said: "I lifted the blanket, I tried to touch what was inside the panty … I was touching [it]” until “she woke up so I left the room." He also mentioned that he went back to assault the woman another time. He refused to apologize for any of these remarks and called "women" craazy for “depriving me of my freedom of expression. You criticise every sentence or word I say, that is my freedom to express myself.” People from young ages get exposed to Duterte’s rhetoric thus such behavior and attitude might be normalized. Everyone has to know the limits of what they will do and say especially considering that he is the most powerful man in position. Ignorance is not an excuse. In here, Duterte is the manifestation of a misogynistic and sexist administration. (Photo from: The Manila Times, 2019)




Silencing of Critics and Dissent

Duterte sought to silence his critics via various means. His most prominent critic, Senator Leila de Lima, remained in detention on politically motivated drug charges. In May, the Philippine Supreme Court took unprecedented action to remove Chief Justice Maria Lourdes Sereno, apparent reprisal for her criticism of Duterte’s “drug war” and other abusive policies. He also ordered the arrest of Trillanes in 2018, after revoking his amnesty for his involvement in unsuccessful military uprisings years ago. Trillanes is retiring at the end of this month and remains free. Our nation is a dangerous country for journalists. Our county ranked 136th out of 180 countries in Reporters Without Borders' global press freedom index since Duterte has sworn into office. It did not come as a surprise anymore when Maria Ressa, the CEO of Rappler, was declared guilty of committing cyber libel even though it was a non-case from the start. Duterte accused the network of being biased because the network broadcasted a negative ad of him instead of airing his ad during his presidential campaign. Duterte also called ABS-CBN “published trash” and claimed that he will see to it that ABS-CBN is out. It was all a familiar sight, weak grounds and bad blood between the president. (Photo from: Angelo Lopez - Association of American Editorial Catoonists, 2020)




Fascist Regime

A fascist leader is (a) a charismatic individual with strong inclinations toward authoritarian rule who (b) derives his or her strength from a heated multiclass mass base, (c) is engaged in or supports the systematic and massive violation of basic human, civil, and political rights, and (d) proposes a political project that contradicts the fundamental values and aims of liberal democracy or social democracy. Fascism pushes a narrative wherein the government claims they are doing a good job yet being deaf to the people's needs which happens today like how they ignore our pleas for mass testing. They also push a dichotomus idea where it's either the people are on they're side or not which can be very harmful considering that we're a democratic country where people should have the right to express themselves. The president also has tantamount disregard for human lifes as seen on his EJKs. There is also blatant misinformation like how the ATL would be good for the people and anyone who opposes them would be considered as communists. This would now lead to conservative values such as outright sexism, misogyny, homophobia, etc. that would be defended by his followers through ad-hominems and victim-blaming. Conservative values could also cultivate a culture of hyperviolence and authoritarian stance, blatant anti-poor sentiment and repression of the working class. (Photo from: Bulatlat, 2017)




Anti-Terrorism Law

The Senate, on Feb. 26, passed on third and final reading Senate Bill 1083, or the “Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020,” a measure that aims to update and repeal some provisions of the Human Security Act (HSA) and “strengthen” the country's capability to combat terrorism. anyone with authority can legally detain you for 24 days if they feel like it. Acts that could get you arrested includes damaging property, endangering the lives of others, being accused of planning something against the government, joining rallies that may "endanger the lives of people", sharing phrases/sentences on social media that may fall under the definition of "terrorism", etc. As we can see the law's definition of terrorism is very vague and this is what is very dsangerous as it may restrict the freedom of speech, freedom of the press, right to assemble peacefully to address grievances, etc. The measure also allows regional trial courts to outlaw an organization as terrorist at the requests of even foreign and supra-national jurisdictions. It likewise removes the compensation for persons wrongfully detained. As Senator Pangilinan said, "Without respect for rights, there can never be security." (Photo from: Glyz De Vera - In Media Res, 2020)




Absence of Concrete Plan Against COVID-19

After 10 days of WHO declaring public emergency, the first reported case on the Philippines was reported. Duterte decided it was a justifiable idea to install police and military instead of implementing a travel ban. By January 26, 1.4 million worth of Filipino-made are shipped to Wuhan and few days after, Panelo said "How can we give facemasks [to the Filipinos] when there's none?" Duterte also assured the public that there is nothing to worry about. Mass testing was also denied by the DOH on March 20. Instead of addressing the issue of COVID-19 as a public health concern, Duterte utilizes full police and military force on the false basis of peace and order. Meanwhile, high-ranking public officials (like Senator Pimentel) who seriously violate protocols are given the benefit of the doubt. The government also decided to go against competent local executives (Mayor Vico Sotto and VP Leni Robredo) instead of focusing on disaster mitigation, public health, and the welfare of the Filipino people. Up until now, there is still no concrete plan. With 300K+ cases, PPEs are already running out, marginalized people are starving, and doctors and nurses are working 24/7 with a low pay.
